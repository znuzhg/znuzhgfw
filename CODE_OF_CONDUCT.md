## Our Pledge
We as community members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability (visible or invisible), ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, safe, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:
* Demonstrating empathy and kindness
* Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
* Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
* Focusing on what is best for the overall community

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:
* Sexualized language, imagery, or inappropriate sexual attention
* Trolling, insulting comments, or personal attacks
* Harassment (public or private)
* Publishing private information such as physical or email addresses without explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
* Encouraging or promoting illegal hacking or unethical security testing

## Enforcement Responsibilities
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate action, including moderation, when behavior is inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

They have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Enforcement Guidelines  
Project maintainers will follow these guidelines when handling violations:

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact:** Minor inappropriate behavior or unprofessional communication.  
**Consequence:** Private written warning explaining why the behavior was inappropriate.

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact:** A violation after a previous warning or a more significant incident.  
**Consequence:** Formal warning and temporary restrictions from interacting with impacted individuals.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact:** Serious or repeated violations.  
**Consequence:** Temporary ban from participation in community spaces.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact:** Sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression toward groups or individuals.  
**Consequence:** Permanent removal from the community.
